No, I gave up on Twitter, Facebook, Istagram and all the other ones. We use these networks more and more to settle our conflicts and less and less to communicate with each other. This is very unfortunate for myself. But above all, this is a terribly inefficient use of technology. 

## What about my professional life?

Before things really took off for me, I had a lot of jobs. I worked as a painter's helper, I delivered and assembled furniture, and I was a system administrator in a courier company. Yes, I also worked there as a courier when the traffic jam paralyzed the city again. 

### Customer Support 

In the middle of the economic crisis of 2009, I found a job as a customer supporter. Hard times, hard job. But I learned a lot. Whether at **2nd level**, in the **back office**, or when **processing fault tickets**, I was able to learn a lot.

But many interests and talents were also awakened here.

I moved to a department where I transformed content from given templates (e.g. Word documents, Power Point presentations and even Excel sheets) into HTML / CSS content. I had my first brief look into coding and content creation and I liked it.

### Customer Support Expert

I switched to [Jimdo](https://www.jimdo.com). Customer support, coding, data. Here, everything that interested me came together. In addition, there was still a start-up atmosphere here. If you love that, you can really pick up a lot here. 

As a customer support person, I have matured here. But I also had the opportunity to get a taste of other departments and tasks. Plenty of room to grow.

* **Social Media Support**: Uhh, the guy without Facebook and Twitter is doing that? Yes, and it was fun and I did it very well. 
* **User Journey**: Who is better suited to create the first real User Journey for the company? If you create these with the knowledge from all the support requests, you probably get the best insights into the pain points of the customers. And to answer the question still. I was really well suited for it.

* **GDPR Task Force:** Imagine the EU gives you 2 years to prepare for a drastic change in the data protection set. Now imagine further that you oversleep 729 days out of these 730 days. Then you need supporters to create a lot of tickets in a really short time (I'm talking about 140 tickets per day and per person). This happened everywhere in Europe at the same time. Data protection can be exhausting.

* **Affiliate / Expert Support:** If you have business clients that you value, that bring you lots of customers, and that advertise for you, they deserve the best support you have to offer. That included me, by the way. 

* **Billing & Payment:** You very rarely have the chance to form a team. In this team you can bring all your knowledge and skills. The colleagues are so great, you even start programming tools for them in your spare time. You provide data and insights to other departments through your work. 

When you have something like that, the work is fun, you learn a lot and you grow and grow. That kind of thing is rare, but valuable. 

## And then this ...

But then, one day, the whole world was sent into lockdown. I and many, many other people were suddenly working from home. Even though life came to a standstill, it had to go on. I also decided that I didn't want to stop in my development. 

On the very first day at the home office, I made a little deal with myself: 

**The time I spent on the train to work, I now use to learn**

This deal was sacred to me. So I sat down every morning after breakfast and studied. My favorite learning tools were the many online academies that are out there: [Codecademy](https://www.codecademy.com), [Udemy](https://www.udemy.com/de/), [FreeCodeCamp.org](https://www.freecodecamp.org/), [LinkedIn Learning](https://www.linkedin.com/learning/) and many more, the curvew was loooong.  

And what have I learned here? Everything I always wanted to know and be able to do!

* **Web Developmen:** So far I have always done HTML / CSS and Java Script only "a little bit and on the side". But if you want to understand how the Internet really works, there is much more to it. Thanks to Codecademy I was able to learn everything a web developer needs to know. I can really recommend this Career Path.

* **Data Science:** because this programming language is easy to learn, but the possibilities are endless. Data, because at some point in Support you are definitely interested in it. Plus, there was still so much curfew and subscription left at Codecademy, so I worked my way through that Career Path as well. 

* At the end of the two courses, I finally had the skills and background knowledge I always wanted. I can design my own website, I can process, evaluate and analyze data. And I simply understand many things better now. 

*And what did I learn on the other platforms?*

My tip for self-study: Try out different platforms. Sometimes you simply understand a topic better when the form of presentation changes. It's worth constantly testing your newly acquired knowledge. The saying is old but true: You never stop learning.

## The other life

But life is not only about working and learning. At least, it shouldn't. Although learning and programming is a lot of fun, work and free time are mixed here, I still do other things.

* **Books:** The classic when it comes to leisure activities. I love books and I'm sure they love me too. Essentially, I have two favorite genres: science fiction and historical novels. Basically, anything that doesn't take place in the here and now, because that's exhausting enough. But I also like history books. My collection on the Roman Empire is impressive. At least for me.

* **Photography:** I have been taking pictures for over 10 years. This has become only an additional hobby. I have taken a camera with me on my many hikes at some point. Originally I wanted to make pictures of my hometown freely available with this new hobby, because 10 years ago there were very few of them. In addition, I also wanted to document how my city is changing. I never became a real professional. But I didn't want that either. I just wanted to capture the beautiful moments of my city. 

* **Sport:** Weight training, jogging, hiking. I do sports every day. I just need it as a balance to the home office, studying, working and everything that is not really good for the body. By the way, I was completely unathletic as a child. It was love that brought me to sports. Romantic, isn't it? Yes, but definitely healthy.

* **Gaming**: I like to play on the PC and my console. But with all the other hobbies and activities, this hobby gets the least time. I just don't have enough of it. It feels like slowly saying goodbye to the best friend of your childhood and youth. You slowly become estranged. But as long as I can, I'm sticking with it.
